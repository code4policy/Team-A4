The Future of the Space Industry

Though highly uncertain, huge room for growth

Though it is hard to predict the future space industry since it all depends on technological breakthrough, there is significant room for growth in the space industry, supported by the rising demand for broadband and falling cost of access to space. Morgan Stanley predicts that the industry will be going to 1.1 trillion dollars in 2040. Bank of America Merrill Lynch is more ambitious, projecting 1.4 trillion-dollar market size in 2030.

Future Section Issues

  1. The demand of broadband rose historically with room to rise further
  2. The cost of access to space fell historically with room to fall further

    3. 1. The demand of broadband rose historically with room to rise further

    The global internet penetration rose significantly from 2009 to 2019 (compare graphs). Though the Internet use has matured in North America and Europe, South Asia and Sub-Saharan Africa have a huge potential to increase the number of people using the Internet.

    Also, there will be additional capacity need arising from the development of autonomous cars, Internet of Things, and artificial intelligence. In particular, commercialization of autonomous cars would require significant increase in bandwidth since they are expected to generate extremely huge data.

    In addition, public spending on space will grow for national security reasons. The magnitude would be modest compared with the private sector, constrained by the budget constraint. The prediction by Morgan Stanley shows that the United States would continue to be the largest contributor, while China would increase its presence.

    2. The cost of access to space fell historically with room to fall further

    Conclusion

    • The demand for satellites is largely pulled by demand for the services they provide, rather than by the aspirational endeavors of the beginnings
    • It is therefore likely that as technological progress continues unabated, more and more services will be available through satellites, facilitated by falling costs of access to space
    • The combination of those trends means that the industry will continue growing through a combination of increased demand and better service
